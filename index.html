Lodestone Content

This page will link you to some of the seminal articles, posts, videos, podcasts, etc. around data mesh. We want this to serve as a jumping off point, not the end-all, be-all. As always, feedback is welcome.

An obvious piece is by Zhamak Dehghani (LinkedIn; Twitter), who created the concept of data mesh, with the introduction of the data mesh concept from May 2019 here.

I think a great next step is a post by Sven Balnojan (LinkedIn) called Data Mesh Applied. It walks through how you might actually put together a data mesh. There are a few very key concepts Sven covers, one of which is "domain teams usually consider their data a byproduct that they donâ€™t really own it". Think of data mesh as being similar to DevOps, where development teams have to care much more about the operations of their applications than they did previously. Data mesh isn't DataOps but it might be DevDQ (data quality) or DevDU (data usability). I am hoping for DevDUX (data user experience) for bad duck puns...

Zhamak updated her thinking about the data mesh in another post called "Data Mesh Principles and Logical Architecture" on Martin Fowler's blog here in December 2020; she drills furhter down into key pillars of the data mesh concept: a self-serve data platform, data as a product, domain ownership, and federated governance.

Here is a great conversation between Zhamak and Sam Ramji of DataStax (LinkedIn; ran Google's Kubernetes business and introduced data mesh concept at Autodesk) on Sam's Open Source Data podcast. It is more of a general chat about data mesh without being so much of a brain dump so you can absorb it a bit easier; link

Zalando, a large fashion retailer in Europe, talks about their journey to data mesh in this 30min presentation. Starting at 8:34, they specifically mention the concept of poor data ownership/data as a by-product Sven talked about above as a big pain point.
